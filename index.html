INTELLIGENT SYSTEM TO ASSISTS FARMERS TO IDENTIFY SPREAD OF DISEASES AND PESTS' PROBLEMS IN BANANA CULTIVATION

This research project emphasizes the significance of agriculture, particularly banana cultivation, as a vital source of food and income for developing nations like Sri Lanka. Despite its widespread growth, most bananas are cultivated on small farms, primarily for local consumption. However, pest and disease attacks pose significant challenges to banana production, often leading to reduced yield and quality.

The identification of these issues is hindered by limited expertise and traditional methods. To address this, the project aims to develop an intelligent system to assist farmers in detecting and managing pests and diseases in banana cultivation. This system will encompass pre- and post-harvest practices, providing solutions for sustainable and efficient banana farming.

Literature Review

Banana farming is a significant global agricultural practice due to its widespread consumption and economic value. In Sri Lanka, with its tropical climate and fertile lands, banana farming holds considerable significance. The industry supports the livelihoods of smallholder farmers, provides job opportunities, income generation, and food security in rural areas.

Research Gap

Banana cultivation is an essential source of food and income for millions of people worldwide. However, it is also associated with several problems that can significantly impact the yield and quality of banana crops. Most commercially grown bananas are clones of the Cavendish variety, which makes them vulnerable to diseases and pests. A lack of genetic diversity in banana crops can also reduce their ability to adapt to changing environmental conditions.

Research Problem

Agriculture, along with its associated sectors, is without a doubt the world's largest source of livelihood. Plant disease is a major concern in agriculture today because it lowers food production and quality. Consequently, it is important to identify plant diseases at an early stage. Currently, banana plants are grown in tropics and subtropics all over the world. Asia has the largest production areas, accounting for 44% of global volume.

Research Objectives

The aim of this mobile app is to provide solutions to the problems that occurs in banana cultivation. This app will use Image processing and machine learning techniques to analyze images of banana plants and fruit, comparing them to a vast Dataset of known banana diseases, pests, and varieties. By simply taking a photo with their smartphone, users will be able to receive instant feedback on the health and condition of their banana crops, as well as recommendations on how to prevent and treat any issues that are detected.

Methodology

An ML-powered mobile app has been developed to tackle the problems associated with banana cultivation. The app uses image processing and machine learning techniques to analyze images of banana plants and fruit and compare them to a vast dataset of known banana diseases, pests, and varieties. The app provides instant feedback on the health and condition of banana crops and recommendations on how to prevent and treat any issues detected
